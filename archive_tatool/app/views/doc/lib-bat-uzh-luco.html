Task Batteries

Language Use and Cognition (UZH-LuCo)

This task battery comprises two tasks each to assess linguistic ability, monitoring, and working memory list-switching.

  • Background
  • Modules
  • How to Cite

Background

This task battery was used to investigate the link between linguistic ability and executive functions in bilingual individuals. The tasks listed here were administered alongside two tasks each measuring inhibition (Simon and Flanker task) and shifting (Shape/Color and Parity/Magnitude Switching task) from the Executive Functions (UZH-EF) Battery. The tasks were originally programmed in Java-based Tatool.

Modules

  • Linguistic Ability (Lexical Decision Making and Word Recognition)
  • Monitoring (Squares and Digits)
  • Working Memory List-Switching (Figural and Numerical)

How to Cite

Oschwald, J., Schättin, A., von Bastian, C. C., & Souza, A. S. (2018). Bidialectism and bilingualism: Exploring the role language similarity as a link between linguistic ability and executive control. Frontiers in Psychology, 9, 1997. doi: 10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01997
